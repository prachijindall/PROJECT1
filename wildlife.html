The Swiss Biodiversity Strategy aims to halt biodiversity loss and ensure the long-term conservation and sustainable use of Switzerland's ecosystems, species, and genetic diversity. Key objectives include maintaining ecosystem services, enhancing habitat connectivity, protecting native species, managing invasive species, promoting sustainable resource use, and raising public awareness

The Wolf Conservation Project in Switzerland focuses on managing and protecting the country's wolf population while addressing conflicts with livestock farming. Key actions include monitoring wolf populations using GPS tracking, implementing preventive measures such as electric fences and guard dogs to protect livestock, and providing compensation for livestock losses.

Bengal Tiger
