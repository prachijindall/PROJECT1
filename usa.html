From the rugged landscapes of Utah’s red deserts to the breathtaking coastal drives of California, the United States is an unrivaled destination for an epic road trip.

Visit awe-inspiring viewpoints on the Grand Canyon, go bear-watching through the wilds of Yellowstone National Park, or collect historic landmarks along the iconic Route 66.

Halloween traces its origins back to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, which marked the end of the harvest season and the beginning of winter. The Celts believed that on the night of October 31st, the boundary between the living and the dead was blurred, and the spirits of the dead could return to earth.

The idea of the melting pot dates back to the early 20th century and is often attributed to American sociologist Israel Zangwill, who wrote a play titled The Melting Pot in 1908. The play depicted the assimilation of immigrants into American society and the blending of different cultures.

New England’s fall festivals are a vibrant celebration of the season, offering a variety of events that highlight the region's charm and traditions,featuring rides, games, and a showcase of regional foods and crafts from all six New England states
