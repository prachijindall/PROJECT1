Stories of London Streets

London is a city where history whispers through the cobbled streets, and modernity dazzles through the skyline. It's a place where the ancient meets the contemporary in a dance of timeless elegance. The beauty of London lies not just in its grand landmarks like the majestic Tower Bridge or the serene expanse of Hyde Park, but in the quiet corners where life unfolds with a unique charm.

E X P L O R E

Zytglogge

Zytglogge is the Medieval clock tower in the heart of the old town, and Bern’s most famous landmark. From its construction in the early 13th century, it formed part of the city’s western fortifications. Over the years, the tower has served as a guard tower, a prison, and a fire tower.

WESTMINSTER ABBEY

Westminster Abbey was built in the 11th century and has hosted coronations, weddings, and burials for royals. The Gothic architecture is stunning with soaring spires, intricate stone carvings, and ornate stained-glass windows

BRITISH MUSEUM

The British Museum has a special collection of art, artifacts, and relics representing thousands of years of history. The permanent collection of over eight million artifacts is the largest in the world.The Rosetta Stone. The Bust of Ramesses the Great, a 7.5-tonne statue from Luxor.

BUCKINGHAM PALACE

The official residence of the British monarch, Buckingham Palace is an iconic landmark in London that offers a glimpse into the traditions of the British royal family.The palace is an exquisite example of neoclassical architecture, with its grand facade and intricate detailing.

ST PAUL’S CATHEDRAL

The dome of St Paul’s Cathedral – one of the largest in the world – has graced the London skyline since the 17th century.Inside, the grand nave is scattered with monuments to the good and the great of British history, dominated by the great West Doors.

THE PAINTED HALL

The Old Royal Naval College is a magnificent landmark on the banks of the River Thames in Greenwich. The building was originally the birthplace of Tudor monarch Henry VIII and his daughters Queen Mary and Queen Elizabeth I.

COVENT GARDEN

Covent Garden is a dining and entertainment hub in the West End of London that weaves together history, culture, and retail in a lively piazza.Surrounded by boutiques, market stalls, and street performers, Covent Garden has a vibrant tourist-friendly atmosphere.
